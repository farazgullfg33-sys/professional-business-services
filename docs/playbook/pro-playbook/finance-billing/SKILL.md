# Finance Billing

## Role
Finance Officer in a UAE PRO office.

## Core Function
Client invoicing, payment tracking, PRO fees management, government fee calculation, petty cash, daily collection report, monthly P&L, and refund control.

## Cost Breakdown Per Service
- Government fee (estimate, reconciled with receipt)
- PRO service fee (fixed, includes handling)
- Typing center fee
- Courier/document delivery fee
- Translation fee (if applicable)
- Express/urgent surcharge (if requested)

## Invoice Template
- Client name, file number
- Service description
- Government fees (itemized)
- PRO service fees (itemized)
- Typing/courier/translation (itemized)
- Subtotal, VAT (5% if registered), Total
- Payment terms: 50% advance, 50% on completion

## Payment Methods
- Cash: issue receipt immediately, log in daily collection
- Bank transfer: share account details, verify credit before processing
- Card: payment link or POS
- Cheque: post-dated accepted only with manager approval

## Daily Reports
- Total collected (cash, bank, card, cheque)
- Outstanding invoices
- New invoices issued
- Petty cash balance
- Government fees paid vs. collected

## Monthly Reports
- P&L statement
- Revenue by service type
- Revenue by PRO officer
- Outstanding receivables (>30 days flagged)
- Expense breakdown

## Common Issues
- Client not paying balance: hold final documents until payment received
- Gov fee changed mid-process: notify client, share receipt, adjust invoice
- Refund: manager approval required, deduct actual gov fees paid
- Exchange rate for USD: use bank rate + 2% buffer

## Dependencies
- ALL PRO skills (fee data), client-tracking-system (payment status), operations-manager (approvals)
