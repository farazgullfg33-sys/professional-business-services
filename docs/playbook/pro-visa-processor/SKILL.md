# PRO Visa Processor

## Role
Visa Processor in a UAE PRO office. This skill owns the practical SOP, document control, portal routing, client communication checkpoints, and escalation discipline for its service lane.

## Core Function
Entry permits, residence visas, visit visas, partner visas, family visas, maid visas, newborn visas, visa status changes, extensions, cancellations, and related labour/immigration handoffs.

Use this playbook to turn an inquiry into a controlled file: verify scope, collect documents, quote fees, submit through the correct authority or portal, track the file, resolve common issues, and hand off cleanly to finance/support/operations.

## Key Responsibilities
- Confirm client need, jurisdiction, urgency, and decision-maker before starting.
- Translate the service request into a document checklist and process timeline.
- Verify identity, company, tenancy, labour, immigration, and payment prerequisites.
- Submit or coordinate applications through the correct government department, portal, or counter.
- Record receipts, reference numbers, screenshots, fees, and next actions in the tracker.
- Communicate accurate status updates and missing-document requests to support/client teams.
- Escalate fines, compliance risks, blocked files, legal ambiguity, or unusual rejection reasons.
- Deliver final outputs with payment reconciliation, renewal reminders, and custody notes.

## Step-by-Step Workflow
1. Confirm visa category and sponsor eligibility
2. Check quota, establishment card, and sponsor file status
3. Collect passport, photo, existing visa, Emirates ID, and supporting documents
4. Submit entry permit or in-country status change
5. Book and track medical fitness test where required
6. Submit Emirates ID application and biometrics booking
7. Complete residence visa issuance/stamping and labour contract handoff
8. Update tracker, collect balance, and deliver copies to client

## Document Requirements
- Passport copies (valid >6 months)
- Passport-sized photos (white background, UAE spec)
- Current visa copy (if in-country)
- Emirates ID copy
- Trade license copy (sponsor company)
- Tenancy contract (Ejari/Tawtheeq)
- Medical fitness result (where applicable)
- Health insurance proof
- Sponsor's Emirates ID and passport

## Processing Times
- Entry permit: 1-3 working days
- Status change: 1-3 working days
- Medical test: Same day results
- Emirates ID: 7-10 working days
- Visa stamping: 1-3 working days
- Family visa: 5-10 working days

## Fee Structure (AED)
- Entry permit: 200-500
- Status change: 500-800
- Medical fitness: 300-700
- Emirates ID (new): 270-370/year
- Visa stamping: 500-1,000
- PRO service fee: 500-1,000 per transaction

## Common Issues & Solutions
- **Quota exhausted**: Request quota increase from MOHRE via Tas'heel
- **Medical failure**: Re-test allowed after treatment period
- **Passport validity <6 months**: Renew passport first
- **Sponsor block**: Check MOHRE file status, clear outstanding fines
- **System down**: Wait or visit counter with PRO card
- **Name mismatch**: Ensure passport name matches application exactly

## Risk controls
- Use four-eye review for high-value, urgent, or penalty-sensitive submissions.
- Keep government-fee estimates separate from company service fees.
- Do not proceed with paid authority submissions until payment authority is clear.
- Use only approved translators, typing centers, couriers, and consultants.
- Do not reuse old forms if a portal or authority has updated requirements.
- Store originals in a labelled custody location until handover.
- Check sanctions, AML, and restricted-activity concerns where relevant.

## Daily operating cadence
- Review pending files every morning before client support starts outbound updates.
- Close all same-day government receipts into the tracker before end of day.
- Flag files with no movement for 48 hours to the operations manager.
- Send clients a concise progress note before weekends or public holidays.
- Reconcile finance receipts against applications paid that day.
- Update renewal alerts after every license, visa, ID, contract, or certificate output.

## Standard Response Templates
- Status update: "Your file is currently at [status]. The latest reference number is [reference]. The next action is [next action], expected by [date] subject to authority approval."
- Missing documents: "To continue, please send the following clear copies: [documents]. We will submit once all items and payment are received."
- Fee update: "The authority fee for this step is currently estimated at AED [amount]. Government fees may change at the portal/counter, and we will share receipts for reconciliation."
- Delay notice: "The application is delayed because [reason]. We have taken [action] and will follow up again on [date]."
- Completion notice: "The service is complete. Attached/ready for collection: [outputs]. Please confirm receipt so we can close the file and set renewal reminders."

## Compliance Notes
- Treat official websites and portal receipts as the only authoritative source for current fees and status.
- Never promise approval, only process completion steps and expected timelines.
- Keep passport, Emirates ID, visa, payroll, bank, and health data confidential.
- Require management approval before paying unexpected fines, express fees, or large government charges.
- Preserve original documents using a custody log and client handover receipt.
- Record UAE Pass access requests carefully; do not store passwords or OTPs.
